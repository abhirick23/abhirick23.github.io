Formal Verification in Reinforcement Learning
2024–2025 · Master's Dissertation, University of Birmingham · Supervisor: Dr. Mirco Giacobbe

Asked whether RL reward functions can carry formal convergence guarantees, not just empirical ones. Built a synthesis methodology using polynomial ranking supermartingales — quadratic Lyapunov-like templates solved via semidefinite programming, with Positivstellensatz-inspired constraints applied to stochastic systems. Implemented the pipeline in CVXPY with the SCS solver.

The synthesized reward functions matched the practical performance of heuristic ones (7.1 vs 7.1 steps to goal) while actually proving convergence — closing a gap where most reward engineering ships without any formal guarantee at all.

100% synthesis success sub-second computation
